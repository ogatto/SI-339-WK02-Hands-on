NHL Data: Analysis

All major sports have accepted analytics as a crucial part of the recruitment, development, and management process of their respective leagues, and so for my final project in SI 370, I decided I would focus on my favorite league and examine the NHL.

I used Python to analyze the distributions of current player ages and nationality demographics.

I then used Tableau to look at the geographic distrbutions of players from USA and furthermore, players from the state of Michigan.

Here I wanted to find a way to visualize how draft round relates to a players salary. I found it interesting to see that players taken in the 8th round have higher salaries.

I then wanted to see if certain statistics could be predictive of overall playoff success. I made a SPLOM plot to see correlations embedded within the larger dataset. I then used a Principle Component Analysis and made a scree plot the plot shows how closely the eventual playoff teams clustered around scoring statistics, and last placed teams clustered around typical poor play statistics.
